Two-Dimensional Sensors employ flat detection surfaces for rapid analysis of biochemical interactions, making them ideal for diagnostics and monitoring applications. In contrast, Three-Dimensional Sensors offer enhanced spatial resolution and sensitivity, allowing for more intricate analyses of biomolecular structures and interactions.
