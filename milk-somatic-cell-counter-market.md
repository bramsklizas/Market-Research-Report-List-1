<p><p>The Milk Somatic Cell Counter (SCC) market comprises two primary types: ultrasonic and infrared. The ultrasonic method uses sound waves to detect somatic cells, providing rapid and accurate counts. It is valued for its speed and efficiency in dairy farms. In contrast, infrared technology measures cell concentrations by analyzing the absorption of infrared light. This method offers high precision and reliability, making it suitable for quality control in milk processing. Both types play a crucial role in dairy management and product quality.</p></p>
