<p><strong>The Prophylactic HIV Drug Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Nucleoside or Nucleotide Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTI)</li><li>Integrase Inhibitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The prophylactic HIV drug market includes various classes of medications aimed at preventing HIV infection. Nucleoside or Nucleotide Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTIs) work by blocking reverse transcriptase, an enzyme critical for HIV replication, thereby preventing the virus from multiplying. Integrase Inhibitors, on the other hand, target the integrase enzyme, inhibiting the integration of viral DNA into the host's genome, which is crucial for the virus's life cycle. Both classes play vital roles in pre-exposure prophylaxis strategies.</p></p>
